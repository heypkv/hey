# Contributing to hey

`hey` is developed and maintained by **HEYPKV INNOVATIONS PRIVATE LIMITED** and
released under the [Apache License 2.0](LICENSE). The source is open for you to
**use, study, run, and fork**.

## We are not accepting external contributions right now

At this stage the project is developed in-house and **not open to outside pull
requests**. PRs opened against this repository may be closed without review.
This will change as the project matures — this document will be updated when it
does, and the terms below are the terms that will apply then.

You are, of course, free to fork under the Apache-2.0 license.

## Reporting problems

- **Security issues:** please report privately via GitHub's *Report a
  vulnerability* (Security → Advisories) rather than a public issue.
- **Bugs / ideas:** feel free to open an issue for discussion. An issue is not a
  commitment that a change will be made or accepted.

## When contributions open (the future terms)

Once we accept outside contributions, every contribution will require **both**:

### 1. A DCO sign-off on every commit

We use the [Developer Certificate of Origin](DCO) (DCO 1.1). You certify the DCO
by adding a `Signed-off-by` line to each commit — `git commit -s` does this
automatically:

```
Signed-off-by: Jane Doe <jane@example.com>
```

The name and email must be real and match the commit author. Commits without a
valid sign-off will not be accepted.

### 2. A Contributor License Agreement (CLA)

Because HEYPKV INNOVATIONS PRIVATE LIMITED offers hey as part of a commercial
ecosystem, contributors will also be asked to accept a CLA (individual or, for
work done for an employer, corporate) granting the rights needed to distribute
and relicense contributions within our products. The CLA and its signing process
(e.g. a CLA-assistant check on pull requests) will be published here when
contributions open.
